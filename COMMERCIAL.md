# Commercial use

Smeldr is licensed under the GNU Affero General Public License v3 (AGPL).

## What this means for you

**Individual developers, open source projects, and companies building their
own sites with Smeldr:** AGPL imposes no meaningful restriction. You can use,
modify, and deploy Smeldr freely.

**Organisations providing a hosted service to others using Smeldr:** AGPL
requires you to release your modifications under the same license. If this
is a constraint, a commercial license is available — see below.

## Future commercial license

When a hosted offering ("Smeldr Cloud") becomes available, a commercial
license will be available for organisations that want to use Smeldr as the
basis of a hosted service without the AGPL obligation.

This will never affect the open source core. The AGPL-licensed framework
you use today will remain AGPL forever.

## Frequently asked questions

**May I use Smeldr for my personal or my company's own website?**  
Yes. No restriction. AGPL imposes no obligation when you are running Smeldr
for yourself or your own organisation. Use it freely.

**May my agency build client websites with Smeldr?**  
Yes, if each client runs their own Smeldr instance. The AGPL obligation (to
release modifications under the same license) rests with whoever operates
the network service — if the client is the operator, the obligation is theirs.
If you are hosting the site on behalf of the client, the same network-service
rule applies to you (see below).

**May I offer Smeldr as a hosted service to my customers?**  
Under AGPL, providing Smeldr's functionality over a network to third parties
counts as distribution. You must release your modifications under AGPL.
If you want to offer a hosted service without that obligation, a commercial
license is available — see "Future commercial license" above.

**May I build a SaaS product on top of Smeldr?**  
Same rule as hosted services. If your SaaS exposes Smeldr's CMS functionality
to third parties over a network, AGPL applies. A commercial license removes
that obligation.

**What counts as a "networked service" under AGPL?**  
Allowing third parties to interact with Smeldr's functionality over a network
(HTTP, API, MCP) — even without distributing the binary. Running Smeldr for
your own organisation (employees, contractors) does not trigger this.

**When will the commercial license be available?**  
With Smeldr Cloud (Phase 3). No date is set. If you need a commercial license
before then, contact us directly — see below.

**How do I contact you about licensing?**  
Email [contact@smeldr.dev](mailto:contact@smeldr.dev). For non-urgent questions,
open an issue tagged `licensing`.
